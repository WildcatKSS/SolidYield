# Regulatory Validation Brief — SolidYield (for external counsel)

**Purpose.** A structured question set to hand to a qualified financial-law adviser (NL/EU)
so they can confirm **whether and how** SolidYield may lawfully operate. **This document is
not legal advice**; it is prepared to obtain it. Passing Phase 1 criterion A depends on
counsel's written answer.

## Product summary (current definition)

- Consumers/businesses deposit funds into a wallet and **lock** a chosen amount for a fixed
  term (3, 6, 12, 24, 36, 60 months).
- They receive a **fixed interest rate, paid monthly**, and the **principal back** at term end.
- **Interest + principal are guaranteed by SolidYield** regardless of realised return (backed
  by SolidYield's own capital — not a state deposit guarantee).
- Deposited funds are **invested** to generate the return; **at the start solely via Mintos**
  (P2P lending), intending to diversify later.
- **One integrated structure** (no separated e-money/investment entity). Revenue = the margin
  between the return earned and the rate paid.

## Questions for counsel

1. Does taking these funds constitute **attracting repayable funds from the public**
   (opvorderbare gelden) that requires a banking licence under the Wft? Under what conditions?
2. Does **guaranteeing** principal + interest change the classification or its severity?
3. Does investing the funds (via Mintos) trigger **MiFID** (investment services), **AIFMD**
   (managing an investment fund / collective investment scheme), or make SolidYield a fund
   manager?
4. Is there a **prospectus** obligation for what is offered to depositors?
5. What **consumer-protection / duty-of-care** obligations apply if offered to retail?
6. The **€100,000 / professional-party exemption**: does it apply here, is it per-participant
   or aggregate, and what are the exact conditions?
7. How do **e-money / payment-services** rules apply to the wallet and money flows?
8. What **AML/KYC** obligations apply, and to whom?
9. What **advertising/marketing restrictions** apply to this product type (relevant to
   distribution)?

## Two scenarios to assess separately

- **Scenario Retail** — open to everyone, low minimum deposit (serves the retail target group).
- **Scenario ≥ €100k / professional** — minimum €100,000 per participant, or
  professional/qualified parties only.

## Structures for counsel to evaluate

- SolidYield holds its **own licence**.
- SolidYield operates **under an existing licence holder** (bank/investment firm) as an
  intermediary/technology layer.
- **Reintroduce a legally confirmed separated structure** (e-money institution + separate
  investment entity), as in the original plan.

## What "pass" for Gate A requires

A **written memo from qualified counsel** stating, per scenario, either: (a) a lawful route
exists, with the licence/registration, obligations, and conditions; or (b) it is not viable
as framed. Anything short of (a) keeps criterion A open and the build blocked (risk R-008,
issue O-007).
